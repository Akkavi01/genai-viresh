TV advertising shows diminishing returns beyond 75,000 spend. Initial ROI is strong but flattens quickly indicating saturation.

Digital marketing channels such as Meta and Google Search continue to scale efficiently with increasing spend. ROI remains positive even at higher budgets.

Print media has consistently low ROI and is not contributing significantly to incremental sales. It is recommended to reduce allocation.

Promotional campaigns drive short-term volume spikes but do not sustain long-term growth. Over-reliance may hurt baseline sales.

Price discounts increase short-term demand but negatively impact brand equity and long-term profitability.

Seasonality plays a key role with peak sales observed during festive months such as October and November.

Distribution gaps in Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities are limiting growth potential despite strong demand signals.

Competitor activity has increased in digital channels leading to higher cost-per-click and reduced efficiency.

Higher frequency in TV ads does not proportionally increase conversions, indicating creative fatigue.

Bundled offers perform better than straight discounts in terms of both volume and profitability.

Search ads have the highest conversion rate among all digital channels.

Video ads are effective for awareness but not for immediate conversions.

Retail visibility and shelf placement strongly influence offline sales performance.

Weather patterns also impact demand, with higher sales observed during cooler months.

Customer loyalty programs have shown positive impact on repeat purchases.
